GST on Health Insurance Premiums in India 2026 — Rate, SAC Code and ITC Eligibility

Health insurance GST is 18%. That much everyone knows. What most people get wrong: who can claim ITC on it, when the split changes between CGST+SGST and IGST, and how car insurance GST differs. Here is the complete picture — with worked rupee examples for policyholders, employers, and developers building insurtech tools.

The rate — confirmed and current

As of September 2025, the GST rate on all health insurance premiums in India is 18%. This applies to:

  • Individual health insurance policies (Mediclaim, family floater, senior citizen plans)
  • Group health insurance purchased by employers for employees
  • Critical illness covers
  • Top-up and super top-up health plans

The SAC code is 997133 — Health Insurance Services. This is the code your insurer uses on every premium invoice. If your premium invoice shows a different SAC code, ask your insurer to correct it — it affects ITC claims for eligible businesses.

SAC 997133 at 18%: Intrastate supply — CGST 9% + SGST 9%. Interstate supply — IGST 18%. For most individual policyholders, the insurance company is in the same state as the policyholder, making it an intrastate supply with CGST + SGST.

ITC on health insurance — who can claim it

This is where most articles get it wrong, and where most businesses lose money by not claiming what they are entitled to.

Individuals — cannot claim ITC

If you are an individual buying a personal health insurance policy, the 18% GST you pay is a cost to you. You cannot claim it as ITC in your GST returns. It is not recoverable. The only tax benefit available is under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act — a deduction on the premium amount, not the GST.

Employers buying group health insurance — CAN claim ITC

This is the exception that most finance teams and CAs miss. Under Section 17(5)(b)(iii) of the CGST Act, ITC is blocked on health insurance except when it is provided as an obligatory statutory requirement — meaning the employer is legally required to provide it.

The Employees' State Insurance Act, the Factories Act, and certain state labour welfare acts mandate health coverage for specific categories of workers. When an employer buys group health insurance to comply with these statutory obligations — not voluntarily, but as a legal requirement — the ITC on the premium's GST component is claimable.

The practical test: Is your company legally obligated to provide this health coverage under any central or state statute? If yes — ITC is claimable on the GST paid. If it is a voluntary employee benefit — ITC is blocked. Have your CA confirm the specific statute that mandates the coverage before claiming ITC.

GST on car insurance — the +900% searched question in 2026

The same 18% GST rate applies to all motor vehicle insurance — car, two-wheeler, commercial vehicle. The SAC code is 997134 (Motor Vehicle Insurance Services).

Frequently asked questions

What is the GST rate on health insurance premiums in India?
18% GST applies on all health insurance premiums. Intrastate: CGST 9% + SGST 9%. Interstate: IGST 18%. SAC code 997133. Source: Notification 11/2025-CT(Rate), effective September 2025.
Can I claim ITC on health insurance GST?
Individuals cannot. Employers providing group health insurance as a statutory legal obligation can claim ITC under Section 17(5)(b)(iii) CGST Act. Voluntary employee benefits do not qualify. Consult your CA to determine if your specific policy qualifies.
What is the SAC code for health insurance?
SAC 997133 for health insurance. SAC 997134 for motor vehicle insurance. SAC 997131 for life insurance. SAC 997132 for term insurance. All at 18% GST except crop insurance (SAC 997139, Nil rated).
What is the GST on car insurance?
18% GST. SAC code 997134. ITC not claimable for personal vehicles. ITC is claimable for commercial vehicles — delivery fleets, taxis, goods carriers — when used directly in the course of business.
